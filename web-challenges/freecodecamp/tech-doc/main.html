Folding in half

Folding paper in half is simple. The following algorithm may be used:

  1. Place the paper onto a flat surface, such as a table or counter.
  2. Firmly grasp two corners of the page which are on the same edge of the page (i.e. not diagonal corners).
  3. Gently these two corners to the opposing corners on the other side of the page.
  4. Ensure each corner is matched as closely as possible with the opposing corner, and then carefully flatten the page such that a crease forms halfway.
  5. Slide a fingernail against the fold to make the crease as crisp as possible.

Congratulations! The page is now folded in half.

Airplaning

With the help of some elaborate folding techniques (see Folding above), a sheet of paper may be transformed into an airplane! To achieve this, simply:

  1. Get a piece of paper.
  2. Fold it into a paper airplane.

Armed with this knowledge, you'll be making delightful children's toys in no time.

Cutting

Another useful technique for altering the shape of a piece of paper is cutting. For this task, scissors are recommended, although a knife may also be used.

  1. Grab some paper and a pair of scissors.
  2. Place the sheet of paper between the two blades of the scissors.
  3. Squeeze the scissors handles together to shear the paper, cutting it.

A piece of paper that has been cut has several uses, such as being two smaller pieces of paper.
